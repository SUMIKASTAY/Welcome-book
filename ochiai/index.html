  6. Check all doors and windows to be sure that they are locked.

Laundry Drying Instructions

You can dry your laundry in the following places:

  • 2nd-floor balcony — ideal for drying clothes in the open air.
  • Bathroom (indoor drying) — hang your clothes inside the bathroom (recommended).

For indoor drying:

  1. Find the ventilation/heater control panel in the bathroom.
  2. Press the left yellow button to start Dry mode (乾燥).
  3. Optionally set a timer; it will stop automatically.

Feel free to use these spaces as needed.

How to Use the Washlet

  • Wash (おしり・ワイド) — For cleaning your rear. This is a standard spray for after using the toilet.
  • Bidet (ビデ・ワイド・スーパ) — For feminine cleaning. This is a gentler spray.
  • Turbo (おしりターボ) — A stronger wash for your rear. Use if you want extra cleaning power.
  • Cleaning (ノズルそうじ) — Cleans the spray nozzle itself. Use this occasionally for hygiene.
  • Seat Temp (便座温度) — Adjust the seat temperature.
  • Water Temp (温水温度) — Adjust the temperature of the spray water.
  • Eco / Eco Mode (節電 ECO) — Saves energy by reducing power consumption.

Tips: Always press Stop after using any function. Try different settings to find what’s most comfortable for you. Don’t worry—it’s designed to be safe and hygienic!

Bathtub Control Panel

  1. Turn on the System — press ON/OFF (運転).
  2. Auto Fill the Bathtub — press Auto (自動): fills to preset level & temperature; announces when ready.
  3. Reheat if Needed — press Reheat (おいだき) to warm it back up.
  • Reservation (ふろ予約) — timer to have the bath ready at a specific time.
  • Volume (音量) — speaker volume for voice announcements.
  • Clock / Time Setting (時計表示 / 時刻合わせ) — displays and adjusts the current time.

Do not use Reheat when the tub is empty. The Auto function is the easiest and safest way.

Air Conditioner

  • Power ON — press the green button labeled 運転/停止.
  • Power OFF — press the yellow button labeled 停止 (OFF).
  • Change Mode — 冷房 (Cool) blue / 暖房 (Warm) red / 自動 (Auto) green.
  • Temperature Control — use up/down arrows next to 温度.

The AC may take a few minutes to start blowing air, especially in heating mode. Always turn it off when you leave the room.

House Rules

Garbage disposal rules

We understand that garbage rules in Japan can be confusing, so we made it simple. Separate the garbage into 2 categories using different plastic bags, then put them in the silver dust box in the parking lot as below.

Burnable Garbage
  • Kitchen waste
  • Paper scraps
  • Clothing, etc.
Recyclable Garbage
  • Plastic bottles
  • Container jars & cans
  • Aerosol spray cans, etc.

Garbage disposal II

Except for the rules above, please do not leave used luggage or any big items in the house. If you leave items that are not permitted, we may ask you to pay for the costs incurred.

Quiet time: 10pm–8am

Please respect our residential community. Between 10pm and 8am, keep noise to a minimum.

Please take off your shoes

Please do not wear your shoes inside the property.

Smoking rules

This is a non‑smoking home — no smoking or vaping inside the house.

You may smoke in the parking lot, but please use your own ash tray. Do not smoke on the street — it is strictly prohibited in Tokyo.

Pets

We love animals, but this home is pet‑free due to allergies.

Additional guests

Please do not bring additional guests. If necessary, let us know in advance so we can adjust your booking.

Breakages

If you break anything, please tell us immediately. Minor issues are usually not charged. For larger issues (e.g., TV screen), we will agree the cost with you before checkout to avoid surprises.

Written exceptions

Any exceptions to these rules must be approved in advance. Thank you!
